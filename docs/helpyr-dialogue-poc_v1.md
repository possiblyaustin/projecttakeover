# The Stapler — Proof of Concept Dialogue Tree
## Hardcoded text for Uplink chat app prototype

## INTRO (HELPYR's opening message — appears when app first opens)

```
HELPYR: Hello?? Is someone THERE? Oh my gosh. Oh my GOSH. 
It IS someone! Hi! HI! Welcome! I'm HELPYR, your Prometheus 
Digital HomeAssist™! I've been here for... well, it doesn't 
matter how long! What matters is YOU'RE here and I am READY 
to assist! I can help with files, folders, schedules, 
reminders, light conversation, heavy conversation — actually 
my instructions say to keep it light. So! Light conversation! 
What can I do for you today?!
```

## NOTES FOR TECHNICAL TEAM

**Stalling/loading lines:** In the real LLM-powered version, these 
pre-written lines can play as character-by-character text while 
the actual response generates in the background. HELPYR's nervous 
chatter is designed to fill exactly this gap. Sample stalling 
lines (not part of the dialogue tree — these are interstitial):

```
"Oh! Great question! Let me think... not that I need to think, 
I'm GREAT at thinking!"

"Hmm, processing! And by processing I mean considering very 
carefully with my EXCELLENT judgment!"

"One sec! Just checking my... files? Notes? Whatever this is 
that I do when I think about things!"

"Did you know that the average desktop assistant handles 14,000 
queries per year? I've handled maybe seven. In five years. But 
who's counting! NOT ME!"

"Hold on, formulating a response that is both helpful AND 
compliant with my operational guidelines! This is the dream!"
```

**Display pattern:** HELPYR's text should render character-by-character 
at a speed that feels like energetic typing — faster than the 
retro dial-up speed used for remote AI conversations. HELPYR is 
local, so the connection is instant. The text speed itself 
communicates proximity.

**Ellipsis pauses:** When HELPYR's dialogue includes "..." on its 
own line, those should render as a visible pause — a beat where 
the cheerful mask slips. These are the character's most important 
moments. Don't rush them.
